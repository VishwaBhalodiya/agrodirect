Plowing Guide

Master the fundamentals of proper plowing techniques. Learn about equipment selection, timing, and methods to prepare your soil for optimal crop growth.

Plowing Equipment

Moldboard Plows

Traditional plows that completely invert the soil. Perfect for heavy residue incorporation and preparing clean seedbeds. Learn about different moldboard types and their specific uses.

Chisel Plows

Ideal for conservation tillage, these plows break up soil without inverting it. Excellent for maintaining surface residue and preventing erosion while improving drainage.

Disc Plows

Perfect for tough or sticky soils. These plows cut and mix residue effectively while creating less compaction than traditional moldboards.

Plowing Techniques

Contour Plowing

Follow the natural contours of the land to prevent erosion and conserve water. Learn proper techniques for hillside plowing and creating effective water breaks.

Deep Plowing

Breaking up hardpan and improving drainage. Understand when deep plowing is necessary and how to avoid damaging soil structure.

Conservation Plowing

Minimize soil disruption while maintaining crop residue. Perfect for preventing erosion and maintaining soil organic matter.
